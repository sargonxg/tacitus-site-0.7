ONTOLOGY // HOW TACITUS REASONS

>> From Raw Emails to a Conflict Graph

Under the hood, Tacitus reduces messy threads and documents into a compact ontology: Actors → Issues → Positions → Interests → Constraints → Options. Each email becomes a set of claims and signals that are attached to this graph instead of living in someone's inbox.

Actors & Roles

People, teams, and institutions are nodes with roles, veto power, and dependency links. Tacitus distinguishes noise from real decision‑makers and spoilers.

Issues & Narratives

Every thread is parsed into issues ("budget", "governance", "security") and storylines ("we are being sidelined", "risk is being hidden"). These become edges in the graph.

Interests & Options

Behind each position, the engine infers latent interests (status, security, recognition) and computes options that satisfy as many of them as possible without breaking constraints.
